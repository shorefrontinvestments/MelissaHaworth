Navigating the Maze: Finding Your Investor Identity in Real Estate

~~ üòÄ üå¥ üè° ~~

 

One of the most common problems I encounter with new want-to-be investors is a lack of focus or what is sometimes referred to as Shinny Object Syndrome. Last month they were looking at small Single Family Homes, two weeks ago it was storage units, last week it was Mobile Home Parks, and today it is Multi-family. If you check back in on this aspiring real estate investor a year from now, it might be Short-Term rentals they are looking into, but they still haven‚Äôt completed a single transaction. Why is that?

Finding good deals and succeeding in real estate is simple, but not easy. Everyone can follow the logic of ‚ÄúBuy Low, Sell High‚Äù and ‚ÄúRent > Expenses = Profit‚Äù it IS simple. Not everyone understands what it takes to learn the market, understand rehab costs, develop relationships with agents, contractors, property management, etc. because it IS NOT easy. If we continuously jump from one strategy to the other, from this niche to that niche, we fail to fully develop the skills and relationships necessary to invest successfully in real estate. In other words, we need to focus on one piece of the puzzle, where it goes, and how it connects to the other pieces before we move on to the next.

How do we know what piece of the puzzle to start with? What type of deals are right for us?

Finding the right deal can be very time-consuming, especially for new investors. There are sooo many properties out there. In the past, we have discussed How to Analyze a Deal, where we explained how to crunch the numbers on an investment property and we have given a lot of our clients our deal analyzer spreadsheet. How to analyze a deal is only half of the answer. More importantly, how do we know what properties we should even put into the deal analyzer spreadsheet?

We know what deals we should be looking at based on our ‚ÄúInvestor Identity‚Äù

What is Investor Identity? Simply put, Investor Identity helps identify what kind of investor we are, what types of assets we invest in, and how we invest in those assets. It acts as a filter to remove a lot of the JUNK in the marketplace before it even gets to your desk to be analyzed. Once you have established your Investor Identity, you will know exactly where to focus!

Here's a guide to help you discover your Investor Identity:

Asset Class: What type of real estate do you want to invest in?


Active or Passive Investing: Are you leaning towards active or passive investment strategies?


Real Estate Strategy: What specific strategy aligns with your goals? (Wholesaling, Fix & Flip, Buy & Hold, etc.)


Target Market: Where do you want to invest? Define your location broadly or specifically.


Type of Real Estate: Commercial or residential? Specify your ideal investment, such as warehouses, Single Family Homes, or Large Multifamily units.


Property Specifications: Define the features of your ideal property, including size, bedrooms, bathrooms, etc.


Rent Range: What does your ideal property rent for?


After Repair Value (ARV) Range: What would it sell for in good condition?


Rehab Budget: How much rehab are you willing to undertake?


Funding Sources: How will you fund your deals? (Cash, Bank Loan, Private Money Lenders, etc.)

This process will guide you towards opportunities that align with your investment goals and save you time by focusing only on deals that fit your Investor Identity. Keep in mind that your Investor Identity can evolve as you gain knowledge, enhance your mindset, and increase your resources.
