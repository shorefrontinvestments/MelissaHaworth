What is Sub 2 Investing? Understanding the "Subject To" Strategy in Real Estate

"Sub 2 Investing" in real estate refers to a unique method of property acquisition where the buyer takes over the existing mortgage on the property instead of obtaining a new mortgage. The term "Sub 2" is derived from "subject to," signifying that the buyer assumes ownership of the property subject to the existing mortgage agreement.

How Sub 2 Investing Works:

Property Discovery: An investor identifies a property that has an existing mortgage.

Agreement: The buyer negotiates an agreement with the seller to take over ownership of the property. This agreement includes the investor's commitment to managing the mortgage payments.

Ownership Transition: While ownership is transferred to the investor, the original mortgage remains in the seller's name.

Mortgage Responsibility: The investor becomes responsible for making the monthly mortgage payments to the lender on the seller's behalf. This step is pivotal in Sub 2 Investing, as any missed payments could potentially lead to foreclosure. Often, a third-party loan servicer is used for this purpose.

Benefits of Sub 2 Investing:

Buyers can reap several advantages through Sub 2 Investing:

Bypassing New Mortgages: Investors can acquire properties without needing to secure a new mortgage.
Negotiated Down Payment: A negotiated down payment may be possible when utilizing this strategy.
Existing Mortgage Terms: Investors may capitalize on favorable interest rates and terms from the property's existing mortgage.


Risks and Considerations:

While Sub 2 Investing offers benefits, it also involves risks:

Loan Ownership: The original mortgage holder's name remains on the loan.
Default Risks: If the investor defaults on payments, the lender could call the loan due in full.
Legal Complexity: Sub 2 Investing entails intricate legal and financial matters. Researching local legal implications, consulting real estate attorneys, and understanding the risks is crucial before proceeding.
