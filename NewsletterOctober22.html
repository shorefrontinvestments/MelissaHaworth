Today, we're excited to shed light on an often overlooked benefit associated with VA home loans‚Äîthe VA loan assumption. If you're a veteran, active military member, reservist, national guard, or a surviving spouse, this might be a game-changer in your homeownership/investing journey.

Understanding VA Loan Assumption: A Brief Overview

VA home loans, born out of the GI Bill of Rights Act in 1944, have long been recognized for their advantages, including no down payment requirements and lower interest rates. However, the hidden gem in this array of benefits is the VA loan assumption‚Äîan option allowing the transfer of mortgage liability from one eligible party to another.

Why Consider VA Loan Assumption?

Unlike a traditional home sale, where you sell the property, a VA loan allows someone else to assume your mortgage's interest rates, monthly payments, and balance if they qualify for the loan. But, why would someone be willing to assume a mortgage?

- Access to Lower Interest Rates: The new borrower can lock in the existing loan's interest rate, potentially lower than current rates.


- Cost Savings: With a minimal closing fee of only 0.5% of the loan amount, significant savings can be realized compared to traditional loans.


- Equity Preservation: Assumption is a viable option in cases of divorce, allowing one party to keep the home's remaining equity.

Navigating the Process: How to Assume a VA Home Loan

Assuming a VA home loan can be a strategic move for both parties involved, but it's crucial to understand the process:

1. Obtaining Permission: Permission from the lender and the VA is necessary. While lender approval suffices in some cases, it's advisable to check with the VA to ensure compliance.

2. Meeting Requirements: The new buyer must adhere to credit and income requirements defined by both the VA and the lender.

3. Ensuring Liability Transfer: The new buyer assumes all mortgage obligations, and any remaining dues must be settled before the loan closing.

Benefits and Considerations: A Balanced View

Benefits of VA Loan Assumption:


- Streamlined Application: No need to apply for a new VA loan, simplifying the process.


- Cost Savings: Lower processing fees compared to first-time buyers.


- Preservation of Entitlement: Original homeowners can restore entitlement after obtaining a release of liability.

Challenges to be Aware of:


- Primary Residence Requirement: The house must be used as the primary residence, not as an investment or vacation property.


- Impact on Future Entitlement: If the new buyer is a civilian, the original seller's VA loan benefits may not be fully recovered.

Protecting Your Interests: Release of Liability

To safeguard future borrowing options, obtaining a "release of liability" before closing is crucial. This legal document signifies that the original owner is no longer responsible for the loan payments, protecting their credit score if the new borrower defaults.

Frequently Asked Questions: Clearing the Air

We've compiled some FAQs to address common queries related to VA loan assumption:

- Can non-veterans assume a VA home loan? Yes, if they meet minimum credit and income requirements.

- Who can be exempted from the VA loan assumption funding fee? Exemptions apply if you receive VA compensation for a service-related disability, among other conditions.

- Can you borrow another loan after a VA loan assumption? Yes, provided your entitlement is not tied to the assumed mortgage.
