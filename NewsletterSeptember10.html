Hi there,

The Panhandle of Florida is a sought-after destination for Short Term Rentals, but insuring your property can be a unique challenge. Standard homeowners insurance policies usually cover owner-occupied properties but not homes rented to guests. Many insurers won't provide short-term rental coverage in Florida, but we work with the best companies who do.

Questions to Ask When Choosing Rental Insurance:

Rental Frequency: Do you rent your home out weekly or monthly?

 

Property Use: Do you occupy your second or vacation home intermittently?

 

Furnishing: Is your second home furnished for short-term renters, or is it empty for long-term tenants?

 

Vacancy Protection: How can you protect your home against perils like vandalism when it's unoccupied?

Understanding Homeowners Insurance:

Homeowners insurance safeguards your financial interests in case your home is damaged or destroyed by covered perils like fires, tornadoes, or hurricanes. However, policies can vary among insurers, so it's essential to review your policy to ensure it meets your needs.

Flood Insurance in Florida:

Florida is no stranger to flooding, and it's essential to understand your options:

Coverage: Flood insurance in Florida covers damage from storm surge and overflowing rivers, including damage to a property's contents.


Cost: The average cost of flood insurance in Florida varies but premiums depend on factors such as coverage type, property location, age, elevation, and the deductible. Mandatory or Not: Flood insurance isn't required by the state of Florida, but your mortgage lender or insurance company may mandate it, especially if you're in a high-risk flood zone known as Special Flood Hazard Areas (SFHAs) starting with "A" or "V". Moderate- to low-risk zones are called Non-Special Flood Hazard Areas (NSFHAs) starting with "X", "B", or "C".
