Exciting News!

Fannie Mae Reduces Down Payment Requirements for Multifamily Homes!

~~ 😀 🌴 🏡 ~~

Hi there,

 

We're thrilled to share some exciting news for new and experienced investors looking to "House Hack"! Fannie Mae has recently made a groundbreaking policy change that could reshape your approach to multifamily property ownership, effective from the weekend after November 18, 2023.

Lowered Down Payment Requirements: More Accessibility, More Opportunities

Fannie Mae will now accept a mere 5% down payment for owner-occupied 2-, 3-, and 4-unit homes. This is a departure from the previous requirement of 15-25% down payments for duplexes, triplexes, and four-plexes. This opens up incredible opportunities for new and experienced investors looking to "House Hack" while enjoying the benefits of homeownership.

Expanded Financing Choices and Easier Approvals

This policy change extends to standard purchases, no-cash-out refinances, HomeReady, and HomeStyle Renovation loans for owner-occupied transactions. Whether you're a first-time buyer or an experienced investor seeking ways to manage high mortgage payments, Fannie Mae's more accessible financing options have got you covered.

The maximum loan amount allowed for these 2-4 unit properties is set at $1,396,800, providing flexibility for larger and more expensive properties. Moreover, the elimination of the FHA self-sufficiency test for 3-4 unit properties means smoother pre-approvals for these types of multifamily homes.

Taking Advantage of Fannie Mae’s Policy Change

If you're considering taking advantage of this fantastic opportunity, the time to act is now. Mortgage loan borrowers can apply ahead of the changes, set to take effect in Fannie Mae’s system after November 18, 2023. This window allows potential buyers to prepare and gather all necessary documentation before the new policy implementation.

For owner-occupant landlords, this policy shift is a golden opportunity to reduce mortgage payments by leveraging rental income. The ability to make a smaller down payment not only makes multifamily homes more accessible but also allows home buyers to gain valuable landlord experience while building equity in their property.

How Does This Benefit You?

In case you missed it, Fannie Mae has just dropped some fantastic news for new and experienced investors looking to "House Hack" - a reduction in their down payment requirements for Primary Home, 2-4 unit properties to just 5%. Previously, a hefty 20% down payment was required for a buyer eyeing a 4-unit property. This 5% down option not only makes homeownership more affordable but also enhances the marketability of higher-valued homes.

While FHA only requires a 3.5% down payment for 2-4 unit properties, it comes with a "self-sufficiency test" for 3-4 unit properties, often necessitating more upfront costs. This Fannie Mae announcement provides a compelling alternative for both new and experienced investors looking to "House Hack."

Here's a quick summary of the benefits:

1. 5% down on 2-4 units
2. PMI that will eventually go away
3. No self-sufficiency test
4. No income limits
5. No first-time homebuyer requirement
6. Must be for primary residences

These changes align with the current conforming loan limits:

- Single Family Homes - $726,200
- Two Unit Homes - $929,850
- Three Unit Homes - $1,123,900
- Four Unit Homes - $1,396,800

And these limits are set to increase in 2024. This Fannie Mae announcement provides newfound flexibility for buyers and offers benefits to sellers as well.

Feel free to reach out with any questions. Let's explore the possibilities this change brings!
