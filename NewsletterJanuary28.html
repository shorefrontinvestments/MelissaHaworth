Understanding Warrantable and Non-Warrantable Condos: A Buyer's Guide

~~ 😀 🌴 🏡 ~~

 

When it comes to purchasing a condominium, one of the crucial factors to consider is whether the condo is warrantable or non-warrantable. In this week's newsletter, we will explore the differences between these two types of condos and address common concerns about buying non-warrantable condos.

Warrantable Condos: Easier Financing Options

A warrantable condo meets specific criteria set by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, the government-sponsored entities that purchase and guarantee mortgages. To be considered warrantable, a condo must meet the following requirements:

- The complex must be completed, with no new construction or ongoing development.
- At least 50% of the units in the complex must be owner-occupied.
- The complex must be in good financial standing, with adequate reserves for repairs and maintenance.
- No more than 10% of the units can be owned by a single entity or individual.
- No more than 15% of the units can be delinquent on their homeowner association dues.

Advantages of Buying a Warrantable Condo

One of the primary advantages of purchasing a warrantable condo is the ease of obtaining financing. Since these condos meet Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac’s criteria, they are considered less risky for lenders. Buyers can benefit from lower interest rates and smaller down payments.

Non-Warrantable Condos: Overcoming Financing Challenges

A non-warrantable condo, on the other hand, does not meet Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac’s criteria for conventional financing. (What that really means is: Lenders are unable to sell the loan on the secondary mortgage market.) Reasons for a condo being considered non-warrantable include ongoing construction, too few owner-occupied units, too many units owned by a single entity, inadequate reserves, or a high percentage of delinquent homeowner association dues.

Disadvantages of Buying a Non-Warrantable Condo

The primary disadvantage of purchasing a non-warrantable condo is the potential difficulty in obtaining financing. Lenders may require a higher interest rate, a larger down payment, or even a cash payment due to the perceived higher risk.

According to a report by the Mortgage Bankers Association, non-warrantable condos represented just 1.6% of all mortgages in 2022.

Can You Still Get a Mortgage on a Non-Warrantable Condo?

While obtaining financing for a non-warrantable condo may be more challenging, it is still possible. Some lenders specialize in these properties and may provide mortgages with certain conditions, such as a larger down payment or a higher interest rate.

According to a report by the National Association of Realtors, 10% of condominium buyers obtained an FHA loan in 2022. FHA loans, a type of government-backed mortgage, can be used to purchase non-warrantable condos.

 

Considerations When Buying Warrantable or Non-Warrantable Condos

Whether considering a warrantable or non-warrantable condo, several factors should be taken into account:

- Financial Health of the Condo Association: Ensure the association has adequate reserves for repairs and maintenance, as well as any planned capital improvements.

- Occupancy Rate of the Complex: The percentage of owner-occupied units can affect financing options, with lenders being less willing to finance complexes with low owner-occupancy rates.

- Management of the Complex: Assess the reputation of the management company or association board overseeing the complex in handling repairs, maintenance, and addressing issues promptly.

- Restrictions and Rules: Review the condo association’s bylaws and any rules and restrictions to ensure they align with your lifestyle and expectations.

- Location and Amenities: Consider the location, surrounding neighborhood, and amenities offered by the complex, as they can impact the property's overall value and resale potential.

Local Perspective on Non-Warrantable Condos

While nationwide statistics indicate that only 1.6% of mortgages are non-warrantable condos, our local real estate market presents a different scenario. In our area, a significant majority of condominiums fall into the non-warrantable category. It's crucial to recognize this local context when considering financing options for your condo purchase.

Why Choose Local Lenders?

Local lenders have a deep understanding of our unique real estate landscape. They are well-acquainted with the prevalence of non-warrantable condos in our market. Unlike conventional mortgage lenders, local institutions are more attuned to the specific challenges and opportunities associated with these properties.

Connecting with Local Expertise

When seeking financing for a non-warrantable condo, we recommend working with local lenders who specialize in our market. These lenders are familiar with the intricacies of non-warrantable condo financing and can provide personalized solutions tailored to our area's dynamics.

To simplify your financing journey, consider reaching out to us directly. We have established relationships with preferred local lenders who excel in navigating the complexities of non-warrantable condo financing. By connecting with us, you gain access to a network of professionals dedicated to making your condo purchase a seamless and well-informed process.

Choosing a local lender ensures that your financing aligns with the unique aspects of our real estate market. If you're ready to explore financing options for your non-warrantable condo, contact us, and we'll connect you with our trusted local partners who understand the nuances of our area.
