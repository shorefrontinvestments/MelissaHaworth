As we step into the new year, it's crucial for vacation rental owners to stay informed about recent ordinances impacting our local rental operations. I wanted to ensure you're up-to-date with critical updates concerning regulations in both Panama City Beach and Bay County's unincorporated areas.

Ordinance 1632: Panama City Beach Regulations

The Panama City Beach City Council's enactment of Ordinance 1632 brings forth essential changes for vacation rental properties within our city limits, effective February 1, 2024. Key highlights include:

Registration and Inspection Requirements: All vacation rental properties must undergo mandatory life safety inspections, with pre-registration opening on November 1, 2023. This ordinance emphasizes safety features and sets occupancy limits based on habitable space.
 
Penalties for Non-Compliance: Failure to comply may result in escalating fines, starting at $500 for the first offense and reaching $1,000 for subsequent violations within a 12-month period, potentially leading to the revocation of the vacation rental certificate.

For the entire Ordinance 1632, click here.

Ordinance 23-18: Bay County's Regulations for Unincorporated Areas

For vacation rental owners operating outside Panama City Beach's city limits, Bay County's Board of County Commissioners established Ordinance 23-18, effective August 1, 2023. Key highlights include:

Short-Term Vacation Rental Certificate: Requirements include maintaining a Short-Term Vacation Rental Certificate issued by Bay County. This ordinance emphasizes safety, necessitating fire safety inspections, specific signage, and compliance with occupancy regulations.
