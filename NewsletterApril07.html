Say goodbye to SQUATTERS!!

We're pleased to inform you about a recent development in legislation that directly impacts property owners' rights, particularly in cases of unauthorized occupation of residential properties. The passage of CS/CS/HB 621 marks a significant step in addressing the issue of "squatters" who unlawfully enter properties and refuse to vacate, infringing upon property owners' fundamental right to exclude others.

Key Points:

1. Immediate Removal Process: The new law establishes a streamlined process for property owners or their authorized agents to file a verified complaint with the sheriff for the immediate removal of unauthorized persons from residential real property.

2. Sheriff's Assistance: Property owners have the option to request the sheriff's assistance in changing locks and removing personal property of the unlawful occupants, with applicable fees.

3. Liability Protections: The law provides immunity to both the sheriff and lawful property owners from liability for any loss, destruction, or damage to property, except in cases of wrongful removal.

4. Criminal Penalties: The legislation introduces new criminal penalties for unlawfully detaining or trespassing on residential dwellings, using false documents, and fraudulently listing properties for sale or rent.

5. Fiscal Impact: While the law may have an insignificant fiscal impact on local governments, it aims to cover costs through fees collected by the sheriff. However, the new criminal penalties may affect jail or prison beds.

Effective Date: Approved by the Governor on March 27, 2024, the law will become effective on July 1, 2024.

This legislation underscores the importance of protecting property owners' rights and ensuring swift action against unlawful occupation. As real estate investors, it's essential to stay informed about changes in laws and regulations that impact property ownership and management.

For more information and updates on legislative developments, feel free to reach out to us. Together, we can navigate these changes and uphold the integrity of property rights in our communities.
