We hope this message finds you well. As advocates for real estate investors, we are reaching out to bring your attention to Senate Bill 280 (SB 280), a piece of legislation that could have significant implications for vacation rentals in our state. We believe it is crucial for you to be informed about the proposed changes and understand the potential impact on your real estate investments.

Overview of SB 280:
SB 280 seeks to revise the regulation of vacation rentals, affecting units in condominiums, cooperatives, or single-family houses that are rented to guests more than three times a year for periods of less than 30 days. The bill introduces various amendments, including the ability for local governments to impose fees, initiate inspections, and enforce compliance with building and fire codes.

Our Concerns and Opposition to SB 280:
While we acknowledge the need for regulations to ensure responsible vacation rental management, we have identified several points within SB 280 that raise significant concerns for real estate investors:

1. Uncapped Registration Fees:

- SB 280 allows local governments to charge "reasonable fees" for registration and inspections without providing a clear cap. This lack of limitation raises the risk of excessive fees, potentially burdening real estate investors with unforeseen financial obligations.

2. Suspensions for Non-Violent Offenses:

- The bill permits suspensions for violations of local ordinances not solely related to vacation rentals. This flexibility may enable local governments to use unrelated code violations as grounds for suspending registrations, presenting a challenge for real estate investors seeking to operate short-term rentals.

3. Legal Hurdles for Registration Reinstatement:

- Property owners are required to navigate the court system to reinstate a denied, suspended, or terminated registration. This not only prevents property owners from operating their short-term rentals but also imposes a substantial financial and time burden.

Take Action:

We encourage you to read the full text of SB 280 [here] to gain a comprehensive understanding of its proposed changes.

Voice Your Concerns:
It is essential to make your voice heard. We urge you to reach out to your local representatives and express your concerns regarding SB 280. Advocating for a fair balance between regulation and the interests of real estate investors is crucial to maintaining a thriving short-term rental market.

Stay Informed:
We will continue to monitor the progress of SB 280 and provide updates on any developments. Stay informed about the legislative landscape to protect your investments.

Thank you for your attention to this matter. Your advocacy is vital in shaping legislation that impacts the real estate investment community.
